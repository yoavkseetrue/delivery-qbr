How this is calculated
Step 1
Per-ticket MTTR
solved_timestamp − created_at_dt
Computed in hours from raw timestamps. Falls back to Zendesk's pre-calculated MTTR column if timestamps are unavailable. Only resolved tickets are included.
→
Step 2
Per-customer MTTR
Σ(ticket MTTR) ÷ resolved tickets
Arithmetic mean of all resolved ticket MTTRs for that customer in the quarter.
→
Step 3
Weighted average (footer)
Σ(MTTR × tickets) ÷ Σ(tickets)
High-volume customers contribute proportionally more to the overall figure. Lower is better.

How this is calculated
Step 1
Sort tickets by date
tickets → ordered by created_at
All tickets for a customer are sorted chronologically by their creation timestamp.
→
Step 2
Gap between consecutive tickets
T(next) − T(prev) per pair
The time between each pair of consecutive tickets is calculated in hours. Requires 2+ tickets — customers with only 1 ticket show no MTBF.
→
Step 3
Average the gaps
Σ(gaps) ÷ (tickets − 1)
The average gap across all consecutive ticket pairs gives the MTBF for that customer. Higher is better — longer gaps mean fewer failures.
